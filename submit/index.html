submissions

The Math Journal Straw Poll is intended to provide early-career mathematicians with an approximate ranking of research journals in mathematics. Perhaps the only important way of ranking such journals is by the opinions of those who may look at journal titles when evaluating job and grant applications.

Poll submissions will be accepted from published mathematicians who have received their PhD and are actively involved in research. See below for instructions on submitting your poll, or the about page for more information.

how to submit

In order to submit a response, please email from an official email address with your zbMATH Author ID (e.g. erdos.pal). The subject of the email should be "mjsp: submission request".

Your zbMATH Author ID can be found by searching your name at zbmath.org/authors. Your email address should be available on your personal or institutional website so that I can verify that it matches your zbMATH ID. I will then email you with a hash to use for your submission. I will try to send the hash as soon as possible, but it may take a few days if I am away from my computer.

Any submissions without a valid hash will be automatically deleted. If multiple submissions have the same hash, only the most recent submission will be recorded.

The primary goal of these procedures is to keep these rankings anonymous, relevant (i.e. from published mathematicians), and free of spam. The secondary goal is to automate my side of the process as much as possible. Thank you for your help!
